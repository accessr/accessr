Introducing Accessr: a free, privacy-first Word accessibility fixer (that runs in your browser)

      

        7 Nov 2025 ¬∑ Accessr
      


      
Accessibility checks for Word files usually mean exporting to PDF, running paid scanners, or handing sensitive documents to a third-party service. Accessr flips that model: everything runs locally in your browser ‚Äî no uploads, no sign-up, no data collection.


      
What Accessr does

      
    
        
  • Detects fake headings and level skips; converts obvious fakes to real heading styles (optional).
    • 
        
  • Flags tiny text and can raise it to your minimum point size.
    • 
        
  • Finds images without alt text; inserts a clear placeholder reminder.
    • 
        
  • Turns ‚Äú‚Ä¢ ‚Äù / ‚Äú- ‚Äù lines into real lists (safe, conservative).
    • 
        
  • Marks likely table header rows so they repeat correctly when exported.
    • 
        
  • Sets language on runs if missing (e.g., en-AU).
    • 
      


      
Accessr is not a WCAG certification service. It prepares Word documents for cleaner export (Word HTML/Mammoth/Pandoc) and faster human review. Final conformance always depends on your published format and context.


      
Why privacy-first?

      
Government, universities and businesses handle sensitive drafts. Accessr never sends your file anywhere ‚Äî it uses Web APIs to open, check and rewrite the .docx directly in your browser. You can even save this page and run it offline.
